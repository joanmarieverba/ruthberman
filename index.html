Ruth Berman, author and poet

A word from the Author

I write speculative fiction, poems (often on sf themes), and occasionally articles (usually on sf themes). When I was in high school, having read about sf fandom and fanzines in Anthony Boucher's Rocket to the Morgue, I informed my classmates Eleanor Arnason (who also became a fiction writer and poet) and Ron Whyte (who became a playwright) that as we were sf fans, we also should be putting out a fanzine. Interests

Author

Ruth has authored or co-authored 2 books, edited 3 books, and translated 2 books. She also has a number of pubished short stories and articles to her credit.

Poet

Ruth has written numerous poems, appearing in a variety of publications. She has received the Rhysling Award for science fiction poetry twice: in 2003 and 2016.

Ph. D.

Ruth has a Ph.D in English (1979) in the area of children's literature.

Oz

Ruth has a long time interest in Oz, and edited Sissajig and Other Surprises, published by the International Wizard of Oz club.

Dr. Who

Ruth has been interested in Dr. Who since the 1960s.

Star Trek

Ruth worked on the set of original Star Trek in the 1960s, producing the newsletter issued by Lincoln Enterprises called Inside Star Trek. She also edited and published the Star Trek fanzine, T-Negative.

Tolkien

Ruth has been involved in Tolkien fandom since Lord of the Rings was first published, and has contributed to The Tolkien Scrapbook. She is a member of The Mythopoeic Society and The Rivendell Group.
